What Height Should You Mount Your TV?

The complete guide to finding the perfect mounting height for every room in your home.

The Golden Rule: Centre of Screen at Seated Eye Level

The single most important rule for TV mounting height is this: the centre of your screen should sit at your seated eye level. For most Australian adults sitting on a standard sofa, this means the centre of the TV should be approximately 100–110cm from the floor.

This minimises neck strain during extended viewing and provides the most natural, comfortable viewing angle. It's the standard we use for every TV wall mounting installation across Brisbane.

Recommended Mounting Heights by Screen Size

The bottom edge of your TV will vary depending on screen size. Here are our recommended mounting heights (measured from the floor to the bottom edge of the screen) for standard sofa seating:

  • 43" TV: Bottom of screen at approximately 85cm from the floor
  • 55" TV: Bottom of screen at approximately 75cm from the floor
  • 65" TV: Bottom of screen at approximately 68cm from the floor
  • 75" TV: Bottom of screen at approximately 60cm from the floor
  • 85" TV: Bottom of screen at approximately 53cm from the floor

Adjusting for Different Rooms

Living Room TV Mounting Height

In most Brisbane living rooms, the sofa is the primary seating. Measure from the floor to your eye level while seated comfortably on your sofa. This is your target for the centre of the screen. For most households, this falls between 100–115cm.

Bedroom TV Mounting Height

Bedroom TVs are typically watched while semi-reclined in bed. Because your eye level is lower and your gaze naturally tilts slightly upward, mount the TV slightly higher than you would in a living room — typically with the centre of the screen at 120–140cm from the floor, depending on bed height and pillow position.

Above a Fireplace

Mounting a TV above a fireplace is popular in Brisbane homes, but it often results in the screen being too high for comfortable viewing. If you choose this position, we recommend a tilting or full-motion bracket that angles the screen downward toward the viewing position. This reduces neck strain significantly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Mounting too high: This is the most common DIY mistake. A TV that's too high causes neck strain and an uncomfortable viewing angle.
  • Not accounting for the TV stand/cabinet below: If you have furniture below the TV, make sure the bottom of the screen clears the top of the cabinet by at least 10cm.
  • Ignoring viewing distance: The optimal viewing distance is approximately 1.5–2.5 times the screen's diagonal measurement. Sitting too close to a large screen causes eye fatigue.
  • Forgetting about glare: Consider window positions and light sources when choosing your mounting position. A tilting bracket can help angle the screen away from reflections.
