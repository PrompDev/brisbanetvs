## Audio should line up with the screen

A good soundbar makes the TV easier to hear, but a poor install can look messy or make the setup harder to use. If the bar sits too low on a cabinet it can block the TV sensor, hide the standby light, collect dust around cables or feel disconnected from the screen. A clean mount keeps the audio visually aligned with the picture and makes the whole wall look finished.

## Built for a clean install, not a rushed wall bracket job Centred alignment The soundbar is lined up to the TV, not just stuck wherever the wall happens to allow. Connection testing We check ARC/eARC or optical audio output and basic TV audio settings before handover. Soundbar mounting is common in renovated living rooms, apartments, bedrooms, rumpus rooms and media walls where a loose speaker on a cabinet would spoil the look. We account for real Brisbane wall types, including Gyprock, VJ timber, brick and mixed renovation walls.

## A tidy soundbar still needs to sound right

Soundbar placement affects more than appearance. If the bar is too low, dialogue can feel disconnected from the picture. If it is tucked behind the TV edge, the speakers can be blocked. If it is mounted too close to a cabinet top, vibration and cable pressure can become annoying. We position the bar so it looks centred and still performs the way the manufacturer intended.
