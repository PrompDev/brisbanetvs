## No dangling HDMI leads

A wall mounted TV should not have a bundle of HDMI leads, power cables and streaming device cords hanging below it. Cable concealment is what makes the finished room look intentional rather than half done. The right approach depends on the wall, the power point location, the devices you use and whether you own or rent the property.

## Built for a clean install, not a rushed wall bracket job Cavity checks We check whether the wall actually has space for the cable route before promising an in-wall finish. Practical device access Streaming boxes, consoles and soundbars are positioned so they remain usable after the cables are tidied. Compliant advice We are clear when a power point or electrical step needs a licensed electrician. Local Detail Common Brisbane cable concealment situations

Newer Brisbane homes often have Gyprock cavities that suit neat in-wall routing. Older homes can have timber boards, mixed linings or surprise noggins that change the route. Apartments may restrict wall penetrations or require conduit. We explain the options before work starts so the finish matches the property.

## The cleanest cable path is not always the hidden one

Good cable concealment balances appearance, access and safety. A fully hidden path is ideal when the wall allows it, but it is not always the smartest choice. If the TV needs frequent device changes, if the wall is solid brick, or if a rental agreement limits wall penetrations, a short conduit run or cabinet route can look cleaner over time than an awkward in-wall compromise.
