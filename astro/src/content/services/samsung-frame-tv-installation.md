## The Frame needs more planning than a normal TV

A Samsung Frame TV is designed to disappear into the room like artwork. That means the details matter. A slightly uneven bracket, a visible One Connect cable, a box placed where it overheats or a TV mounted at the wrong height can spoil the effect even if the screen technically works.

## Built for a clean install, not a rushed wall bracket job Flush alignment We use the mount template carefully and check the wall surface before committing to the final position. Cable route planning The One Connect cable path is planned around wall type, furniture, safety and future device access. Premium handover Inputs, Wi-Fi, basic picture settings and art-mode expectations are checked before we leave. Local Detail Premium Brisbane rooms need a cleaner install

Frame TV installs are common in renovated Queenslanders, apartments, bedrooms, living rooms with artwork walls and architect-designed media spaces. The goal is not just to hold the TV up; it is to make the TV look like it was always part of the room.

## Small details decide whether it really looks like art

The Frame is less forgiving than a standard TV because the whole point is the finish. The wall needs to be flat enough, the mount needs to be positioned accurately and the One Connect cable route needs to be thought through before the screen goes up. If the cable exits in the wrong spot or the box ends up somewhere hard to reach, the install can look clean on day one but become annoying when you add a console, soundbar or streaming device later.
