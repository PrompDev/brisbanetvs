## Why most Brisbane TV installs go wrong on Saturday

Every weekend we get two or three calls that start the same way: *"I tried it myself, something gave way, can you come fix it?"* The TV is usually a 65-inch or bigger, the wall is usually brick or old hardwood-framed plasterboard, and the anchor that failed is usually the same generic plastic plug you find in a 100-pack at the big-box store.

That's not a tools problem — it's a spec problem. The anchor has to match the wall, the wall has to match the bracket rating, and the bracket has to match the TV's real-world weight (not its advertised weight, which in our experience is optimistic about 15% of the time). We've been doing this in Brisbane for over a decade and our job has always been less about "hanging a TV" and more about getting those three numbers to match.

## What a Brisbane TVs install actually looks like

**Pre-install check.** We confirm the TV's VESA pattern and weight, photograph the wall, identify the stud locations or brick course, and flag any powerpoint/aerial issues *before* we show up. Nothing kills a Saturday faster than arriving with the wrong bracket.

**On the day.** Two installers, 60–120 minutes depending on complexity. We mount with a digital level (not a bubble level, which is off by up to 1° on a warped drywall), run cables in-wall if you've asked for it, and test the TV end-to-end — including the soundbar pair, the app login, and the remote programming.

**Handover.** We leave behind a before/after photo pack, the bracket's written warranty, and our direct number for the 12-month workmanship window. If the bracket ever needs to come down (for painting, house sale, whatever) we'll come out and do it for the cost of the anchor repair kit.

## Wall types we install on every week

Brisbane's housing stock is unusually varied for a single city. Here's what we see most often, and what changes per wall type:

**Plasterboard over timber stud (most 1980s+ builds).** Easy-to-medium. We toggle into the stud if it's in the right spot, otherwise we use engineered plasterboard anchors rated well above the TV weight.

**Double-brick (most inner-suburb queenslanders and pre-war builds).** Medium difficulty. Masonry-rated anchors into the brick face, never the mortar joint. Diamond bits to prevent face-spalling.

**Fibro / asbestos-containing walls (plenty of Brisbane's older fibro cottages).** We assess, and on confirmed asbestos sheets we'll either recommend a licensed removalist or fit a free-standing stand instead of drilling. Your safety wins every time.

**Rendered concrete.** Harder than it looks. We hammer-drill through the render into the substrate and use a chemical anchor for anything above 40 kg.
