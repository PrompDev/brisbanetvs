## A Starlink dish is only as good as its install

Starlink hardware is simple to plug in, but a poor mount is where the problems start: a dish bolted to a flimsy bracket, a cable run loosely over the roof edge, or a position that loses signal every time a tree moves in the wind. We treat it as a proper roof and cabling job — secure mount, weather-sealed penetration, and a clean cable route into the room where you want the router.

## What a Starlink installation includes Secure roof or wall mount Dish mounted to a solid structural point with a bracket suited to your roof type — tin, tile or wall — and weather-sealed where it penetrates. Line-of-sight assessment We check the dish has a clear, obstruction-free view of the sky to avoid dropouts from trees or rooflines. Cable route & router setup Cable run tidily into the house, router placed for best coverage, and a speed test on completion. Install Process How we install Starlink Assess the best dish position for clear line of sight to the sky. Mount the dish securely to roof or wall structure with the right bracket for your roof type. Weatherproof the mount and any roof penetration. Route the Starlink cable neatly into the house to your chosen router location. Set up the router, confirm connection and run a speed test before leaving. Best Suited For Who Starlink installation suits in Brisbane

Starlink is ideal for acreage and outer-north properties where NBN is slow or unreliable — areas like Samford , Cashmere , Warner , Eatons Hill and the wider Moreton Bay outer suburbs. It also suits homes with poor fixed-line options, sheds, granny flats and rural workshops.

## Tin and tile roof mounting

We mount on Colorbond and corrugated tin roofs and on tile roofs, choosing a bracket and fixing method that keeps the roof watertight. Where a roof mount is not ideal, we can use a wall or pole mount instead. We assess the safest, most stable option for your specific roof on arrival.
