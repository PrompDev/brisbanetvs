## Wall types and install planning

Rothwell's housing includes established brick homes from the 1970s and 80s, as well as modern townhouse and unit developments that have sprung up near the rail connection. The older homes typically feature standard Gyprock on timber framing with occasional brick feature walls, while newer builds have contemporary plasterboard on steel or timber frames. The suburb's coastal proximity means some homes experience slightly higher humidity, which we factor into our hardware selection.

Every job starts with structure. We locate timber studs where possible, check masonry or rendered walls before drilling, and confirm whether a cavity is suitable for low-voltage cable concealment. If in-wall cable hiding is not right for the property, we recommend a neat conduit or cabinet route instead of making unnecessary holes.
