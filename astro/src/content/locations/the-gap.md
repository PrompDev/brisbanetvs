## Wall types and install planning

The Gap's housing is predominantly well-maintained family homes from the 1960s through to the 2000s. Construction ranges from post-war timber homes on elevated stumps to large contemporary builds on the hillside. The typical The Gap home has brick veneer or timber construction with plasterboard interiors, spacious living areas, and often a dedicated rumpus room or second living space. The hilly terrain means many homes have split-level designs with interesting wall configurations.

Every job starts with structure. We locate timber studs where possible, check masonry or rendered walls before drilling, and confirm whether a cavity is suitable for low-voltage cable concealment. If in-wall cable hiding is not right for the property, we recommend a neat conduit or cabinet route instead of making unnecessary holes.
