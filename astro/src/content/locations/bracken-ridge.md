## Wall types and install planning

Bracken Ridge features a wide mix of housing types — classic 1970s and 80s brick homes with spacious yards, newer townhouse complexes along Barrett Street and Telegraph Road, and more recent estates with contemporary builds. The older homes typically have Gyprock on timber framing with reliable stud spacing, while the townhouses often have a mix of steel and timber framing with plasterboard walls. Some of the established homes also feature brick feature walls that are perfect for heavy-duty TV mounting.

Every job starts with structure. We locate timber studs where possible, check masonry or rendered walls before drilling, and confirm whether a cavity is suitable for low-voltage cable concealment. If in-wall cable hiding is not right for the property, we recommend a neat conduit or cabinet route instead of making unnecessary holes.
