## Wall types and install planning

Keperra homes are predominantly post-war timber houses and solid 1960s–80s brick homes, with renovated plasterboard sections added over time. The result is that a single home often needs two different fixing methods — masonry anchors for the original brick feature wall and standard cavity fixings for the renovated Gyprock. We carry both, so the approach is matched to the actual wall rather than forced.

Every job starts with structure. We locate timber studs where possible, check masonry or rendered walls before drilling, and confirm whether a cavity is suitable for low-voltage cable concealment. If in-wall cable hiding is not right for the property, we recommend a neat conduit or cabinet route instead of making unnecessary holes.
