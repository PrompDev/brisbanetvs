## Wall types and install planning

Mitchelton's housing is mostly post-war timber homes and 1970s brick houses, many now renovated, alongside newer townhouses near the train station. The older homes frequently have mixed framing — original timber studs in one section and modern plasterboard in another — which can shift stud spacing unexpectedly. We always scan the full wall surface so the bracket lands on solid structure rather than just lining board.

Every job starts with structure. We locate timber studs where possible, check masonry or rendered walls before drilling, and confirm whether a cavity is suitable for low-voltage cable concealment. If in-wall cable hiding is not right for the property, we recommend a neat conduit or cabinet route instead of making unnecessary holes.

## Install tip for Mitchelton

Many Mitchelton homes have a renovated extension joined to the original house, and the wall framing often changes right at that join. We find the studs across the whole wall before drilling, because guessing at standard 450mm spacing on an older Mitchelton home is how brackets end up anchored into nothing.
