Fixed vs Tilt vs Full-Motion TV Brackets: Which One Do You Need?

Not all TV brackets are created equal. Here's everything you need to know to choose the right mount for your Brisbane home.

Your Bracket Choice Affects Your Viewing Experience

The bracket you choose determines how your TV sits against the wall, how much flexibility you have in positioning, and even how easy it is to access cables behind the screen. Here's a breakdown of the three main types.

Fixed (Flat) Mounts

Fixed mounts are the simplest and most affordable option. The TV sits flush against the wall, typically 20-50mm from the surface. There's no movement once installed.

Best for: Living rooms where the TV is at eye level when seated and doesn't need to be adjusted. Fixed mounts provide the sleekest, most minimalist appearance.

Pros: Lowest profile, most affordable, cleanest look, very secure, easiest installation.

Cons: No angle adjustment, difficult to access cables behind the TV after installation.

Tilt Mounts

Tilt mounts allow you to angle the TV screen downward, typically 5-15 degrees. The TV still sits close to the wall but gives you some flexibility in the viewing angle.

Best for: Bedrooms where the TV is mounted higher on the wall, or living rooms where the seating is closer to the TV and slightly below eye level. Also ideal for above-fireplace installations.

Pros: Reduces glare, improves viewing angle when mounted higher, still relatively slim profile, more affordable than full-motion.

Cons: No side-to-side swivel, limited cable access compared to full-motion.

Full-Motion (Articulating) Mounts

Full-motion mounts use articulated arms that extend, swivel, tilt, and retract. They offer the most flexibility, allowing you to pull the TV away from the wall, rotate it left or right, and angle it up or down.

Best for: Corner installations where the TV needs to face different seating areas, rooms with multiple seating positions, or situations where you need frequent access to rear ports.

Pros: Maximum flexibility, easy cable access, can be repositioned for different viewing scenarios, ideal for open-plan spaces.

Cons: Extends further from wall (80-120mm when fully retracted), more expensive, more complex installation, slightly more visible.

Which Bracket Size Do You Need?

Brackets are rated by VESA standard (the pattern of mounting holes on the back of your TV). Common sizes include 200x200mm for smaller screens, 400x400mm for mid-sized TVs, and 600x400mm for larger screens. Always check your TV's VESA pattern before purchasing a bracket.

What About Specialised Mounts?

Beyond the three main types, there are specialised brackets for specific situations:

  • Ceiling mounts — for rooms without suitable wall space or where you want the TV hanging from above.
  • Motorised mounts — remote-controlled brackets that can move the TV into position at the touch of a button.
  • Pull-down mounts — designed specifically for above-fireplace installations, allowing you to lower the TV to eye level.
  • Portrait/rotating mounts — for flipping the TV between landscape and portrait orientation.
