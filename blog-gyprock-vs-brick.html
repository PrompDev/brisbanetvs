Gyprock vs Brick vs VJ Walls: Which Wall Type Do You Have?

Understanding your wall type is the most important step before installing a wall-mounted TV. Here's how to identify yours.

Why Wall Type Matters for TV Mounting

The material and structure of your wall determines what mounting hardware is needed, how much weight it can support, and how complicated the installation will be. Using the wrong mounting method can damage your wall or, worse, cause your TV to fall.

Gyprock (Plasterboard on Timber or Metal Studs)

Gyprock is the most common wall type in modern Brisbane homes. It consists of plasterboard sheets attached to timber or metal studs spaced 450mm or 600mm apart. The plasterboard itself cannot support significant weight — the mounting screws must anchor into the studs behind it.

How to identify: Tap the wall — Gyprock sounds hollow. Look for vertical lines or rows of nail pops that indicate stud positions. In newer homes, you may see a textured finish.

Mounting method: We use a stud finder to locate the framing behind the plasterboard, then drill into the timber or metal studs with appropriate hardware. For metal studs, specialised toggle bolts or snap toggles may be required.

Brick Veneer

Many Queenslander homes and 1970s-1990s Brisbane houses have brick veneer construction. This involves a single layer of brickwork on the exterior with timber framing and plasterboard on the interior. From inside, the wall feels like Gyprock, but the exterior is brick.

How to identify: Check the outside of your home. If you see brick from the outside but plasterboard from the inside, you have brick veneer. The brick contributes to structural support but you're still mounting into studs through the plasterboard.

Mounting method: Same as standard Gyprock — we locate and drill into the internal timber studs. In some cases, we may drill into the brick behind for additional anchoring of heavy screens.

Double Brick (Solid Brick Walls)

Some older Brisbane homes and unit blocks have internal double brick walls. These are solid brick walls with render or plaster applied directly to the brick surface. They're extremely strong but require specialist drilling equipment.

How to identify: Knock on the wall — solid brick produces a dense, solid sound rather than a hollow one. There's no give when you push against it. Older homes and ground-floor units are most likely to have this construction.

Mounting method: We use a hammer drill with masonry bits and install chemical anchors or expanding concrete bolts. Brick walls can hold significantly more weight than stud walls.

VJ (Vertical Joint) Panels in Queenslanders

VJ panel walls are a hallmark of Brisbane's iconic Queenslander homes. These are timber boards (originally pine, often tongue-and-groove) that run vertically from floor to ceiling. They're beautiful but present unique challenges for TV mounting.

How to identify: You'll see visible vertical lines where individual timber boards meet. The surface has a distinct rippled texture. VJ walls are common in pre-1950s homes and renovations that preserve the original character.

Mounting method: We find the horizontal noggins (bracing timbers) behind the VJ panels and mount into those. In some cases, we may need to cut the bracket into the panel and reinforce from behind. This is best left to experienced professionals.

Cement Sheet (Fibro)

Common in 1960s-1980s Brisbane homes, cement sheet walls (also known as fibro or asbestos sheeting in older homes) are hard, brittle, and require special handling.

Warning: If your home was built before 1990, the cement sheet may contain asbestos. Do not drill into it without professional testing. At Brisbane TVs, we carry the appropriate PPE and disposal protocols for asbestos-containing materials.
