Should You Wall Mount Your Soundbar? Pros, Cons & Installation Tips

A wall-mounted soundbar completes the clean look of a floating TV. Here's what you need to know before mounting yours.

The Soundbar Dilemma: Shelf or Wall?

Once your TV is beautifully wall-mounted, the next question is what to do with the soundbar. Leaving it on a cabinet below creates a disjointed look, but not every soundbar is designed for wall mounting. Here's how to decide.

Does Your Soundbar Support Wall Mounting?

Most modern soundbars include keyhole mounting slots or threaded inserts on the back panel. Check the user manual or look for:

  • Keyhole slots (two wide-top narrow-bottom openings) on the back
  • Threaded screw inserts (usually M4 or M6 size)
  • A wall-mount template included in the box
  • Mention of wall mounting in the product specifications

If your soundbar doesn't have mounting points, you can use a universal soundbar bracket that clamps onto the soundbar or sits beneath it on a shelf bracket.

Optimal Soundbar Positioning

Soundbar placement significantly affects audio quality. Follow these guidelines for the best sound:

  • Height: Mount the soundbar directly below the TV, ideally with the bottom edge 50-100mm below the bottom of the TV screen.
  • Ear level: The centre of the soundbar should be at seated ear level for optimal audio clarity.
  • Clearance: Leave at least 50mm clearance above and below the soundbar for air circulation and to avoid sound reflection issues.
  • Alignment: Centre the soundbar horizontally with the TV for proper stereo imaging.

Mounting Methods

Direct wall mount: If your soundbar has keyhole slots, we can mount it directly to the wall using appropriate anchors for your wall type. The soundbar sits flush against the wall.

Bracket mount: For soundbars without direct mounting points, we use universal brackets that attach to the wall and cradle or clamp the soundbar. These often allow some angle adjustment.

Combined TV/soundbar bracket: Some TV brackets include a soundbar mounting bar that attaches to the TV mount itself, creating a unified floating look. This is the premium option.

Wiring and Cable Management

Soundbars need power and an audio connection (HDMI ARC/eARC, optical, or Bluetooth). For the cleanest look:

  • Run the HDMI or optical cable through the wall alongside your TV cables
  • Use a power bridge kit to conceal the power cable in-wall (may require an electrician)
  • If running cables externally, use a dual-channel raceway to keep TV and soundbar cables together

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Mounting too high: Sound should come from ear level, not near the ceiling above the TV.
  • Blocking the TV sensor: Make sure the soundbar doesn't block your TV's infrared receiver.
  • Ignoring ARC compatibility: Ensure your TV and soundbar support HDMI ARC/eARC for single-cable control.
  • Poor ventilation: Soundbars generate heat. Don't enclose them in tight spaces.
