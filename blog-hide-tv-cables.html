How to Hide TV Cables in the Wall: The Brisbane Homeowner's Guide

Dangling cables are the number one eyesore after a wall-mounted TV. Here's how to achieve that clean, professional look.

Why Cable Concealment Matters

Even the most beautifully mounted TV looks unfinished with cables hanging down to the power point. Professional cable concealment transforms the appearance of your installation and protects cables from accidental damage, pets, and children.

Option 1: In-Wall Cable Concealment (Best Results)

In-wall concealment involves cutting small openings in the wall behind the TV and near the power point, running cables through the wall cavity, and installing brush plates or wall plates for a clean finish. This is the gold standard for cable management.

How it works: Two small holes are cut in the wall — one behind the TV and one near the power point. Cables are fed through the cavity using fish tape. Brush plates or keystone wall plates cover the openings.

Best for: Gyprock and plasterboard walls. Requires professional installation to ensure compliance with Australian electrical safety standards (AS/NZS 3000). Some in-wall cable solutions require a licensed electrician if they involve power outlets.

Pros: Completely invisible cables, professional finish, protects cables, increases home value.

Cons: Requires wall drilling, more expensive than surface solutions, may need sparky for power relocation.

Option 2: Surface-Mounted Cable Channels

Cable channels (also called cable raceways or trunking) are plastic or metal channels that stick to the wall surface and conceal cables inside. They can be painted to match your wall colour.

Best for: Brick walls where in-wall concealment isn't practical, rental properties where you can't modify walls, or quick DIY installations.

Pros: No wall cutting required, DIY-friendly, removable, works on any wall type.

Cons: Visible channels (even when painted), less sleek than in-wall, can collect dust.

Option 3: Behind-the-TV Storage

Some TV brackets come with built-in cable management features, such as cable clips, channels on the bracket arms, or removable covers that hide cables behind the screen. These work well for the cables immediately behind the TV but don't solve the problem of cables running down the wall.

What About Power Points?

If your power point isn't located directly behind the TV, you'll either need to use a surface-mounted channel to reach it, or have a licensed electrician install a new power point behind the TV. At Brisbane TVs, we can arrange for a qualified electrician to relocate or add power points as part of your installation.

Brisbane-Specific Considerations

Brisbane's climate and housing styles present unique challenges for cable concealment:

  • Queenslander homes: VJ panel walls are difficult to cut cleanly for in-wall solutions. Surface channels are often the best option.
  • Brick veneer: In-wall runs are possible through the plasterboard layer but cannot penetrate the brick.
  • Double brick: In-wall concealment is not possible. Surface channels or furring channel solutions are used.
  • Humidity: Brisbane's humidity can affect adhesive-backed channels. We use mechanical fasteners for long-term reliability.
