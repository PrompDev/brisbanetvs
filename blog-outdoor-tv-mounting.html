Can You Mount a TV Outside? Outdoor TV Installation in Brisbane

Queensland's beautiful climate makes outdoor entertainment areas a staple of Brisbane living. Here's everything you need to know about outdoor TV mounting.

Yes, You Can Mount a TV Outside — With the Right Setup

Brisbane's subtropical climate means year-round outdoor entertaining, and more homeowners are looking to bring the big game or movie night to their patio or verandah. But outdoor TV mounting requires careful planning to protect your investment from sun, rain, humidity, and insects.

Indoor TV vs Outdoor TV: What's the Difference?

Standard indoor TVs are not designed for outdoor use. Even under a covered patio, humidity, temperature fluctuations, and dust can damage an indoor TV within months. For outdoor installations, we recommend:

  • Outdoor-rated TVs: Designed with sealed enclosures, brighter screens (1000+ nits) for daytime viewing, anti-glare coatings, and corrosion-resistant components.
  • Outdoor TV covers: For occasional use of indoor TVs in sheltered areas, a high-quality weatherproof cover can extend the TV's life.
  • Full-sun TVs: For exposed areas, specialised full-sun outdoor TVs can handle direct sunlight and temperatures up to 50°C.

Shelter Requirements

Even outdoor-rated TVs need protection from direct rain and sunlight:

  • Covered patios and verandahs — Ideal locations, providing shelter while maintaining the outdoor feel.
  • Pergolas with louvered roofs — Good if the roof provides full coverage when closed.
  • Open decks — Require a weatherproof TV enclosure or cabinet.
  • Poolside — Corrosion-resistant mounting hardware is essential due to chlorine exposure.

Mounting Considerations

Outdoor mounting is different from indoor installation in several ways:

  • Full-motion brackets are recommended — They allow you to adjust the screen to avoid glare and can be pushed closer to the wall when not in use.
  • Stainless steel hardware — All brackets, bolts, and anchors should be stainless steel to prevent rust in Brisbane's humid climate.
  • Secure anchoring — Outdoor walls may be brick, concrete, or timber. Each requires different anchoring methods.
  • Power and signal — Outdoor power points must be weatherproof and installed by a licensed electrician. Plan your cable runs before mounting.

Cable Management for Outdoor Installations

Cables are especially vulnerable outdoors. Use weatherproof cable glands, silicone sealant around entry points, and marine-grade cable ties. Consider running cables through conduit for additional protection against Brisbane's storms and wildlife.

Glare and Viewing Angles

Outdoor viewing conditions are very different from indoor. Brisbane's bright sun means you'll want:

  • A TV with at least 1000 nits brightness for daytime viewing
  • Anti-glare screen coating
  • A full-motion bracket for angle adjustment throughout the day
  • Consider the sun's path when choosing the mounting location
